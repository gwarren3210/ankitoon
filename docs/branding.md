# Toonky Brand Guidelines

## Mission Statement

**Toonky transforms time spent reading webtoons into Korean language progress.**

We believe that the best learning happens when you're motivated by content you actually care about. For intermediate Korean learners who want to read webtoons in their original language, Toonky provides the exact vocabulary you need, when you need it - turning guilty pleasure reading time into genuine language acquisition.

## Vision (5 Years)

Toonky becomes the default free tool for Korean learners who love webtoons. Through aggregated learning data, we intelligently recommend optimal learning paths and suggest webtoons matched to your vocabulary level. Reading webtoons in Korean will be an accessible goal for any dedicated learner.

## Brand Essence

**The bridge between effort and understanding.**

Toonky is purposeful, focused, and honest about the work required. We respect our users' motivation and intelligence. We don't gamify for the sake of engagement - we create tools that deliver real results. Every feature serves one goal: helping you understand the stories you want to read.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary: Deep Navy** `#1A2332`
- Conveys seriousness and focus
- Reading-friendly, works in dark mode
- Professional without being corporate

**Accent: Electric Blue** `#0099CC`
- Energy and progress
- The "aha moment" color
- Highlights, progress bars, CTAs
- WCAG AA compliant on white backgrounds
- Note: For decorative elements or large text, brighter `#00D9FF` can be used

**Supporting Colors:**
- White `#FFFFFF` - Primary background
- Light Gray `#F5F7FA` - Secondary backgrounds
- Dark Gray `#2D3748` - Body text
- Medium Gray `#718096` - Secondary text

**Usage:**
- Navy for headers, primary text, navigation
- Electric blue sparingly for progress, highlights, key actions
- High contrast for Korean vocabulary display
- Dark mode: Invert with electric blue maintaining prominence

### Typography

**Primary Font: Sans-serif system stack**
- High legibility for Korean and English
- Medium to semi-bold weights preferred
- Clear hierarchy: Korean vocab stands out from translations

**Hierarchy Example:**
- Korean vocabulary: Large, bold, navy
- Romanization: Small, medium gray
- English translation: Medium, dark gray
- UI text: Clean, readable, appropriate sizing

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** - Reading comprehension is the goal
2. **Vertical flow** - Honor webtoon reading patterns where appropriate
3. **Generous spacing** - Never cluttered, always breathable
4. **Progress made visible** - Substantial indicators (bars, counts, milestones)
5. **Dark mode first** - Optimized for reading sessions

## Brand Voice

### Personality Traits
- **Encouraging but honest** - Acknowledges effort required
- **Focused and purposeful** - Every word serves a function
- **Quietly confident** - Results speak louder than hype
- **Respectful** - Treats users as serious learners

### Voice Guidelines

**We sound like:**
- A knowledgeable training partner
- Someone who believes in you but won't sugarcoat the work
- A friend who shares your webtoon obsession but keeps you on track

**We DO:**
- Use clear, direct language
- Celebrate milestones authentically
- Show progress with numbers and facts
- Address users by username when appropriate
- Connect study sessions to reading goals

**We DON'T:**
- Use excessive exclamation points or emojis
- Artificially hype mundane actions
- Use corporate jargon or academic language
- Guilt or pressure users
- Over-explain or talk down to users

## Key Brand Moments

### 1. The Study Session
**Feeling:** Focused, efficient, like a good workout
**Design:** Clean flashcard interface, progress bar visible, minimal distractions
**Voice:** "22 cards remaining" not "You're almost done!"

### 2. The Unlock
**Feeling:** Earned satisfaction
**Design:** Clear threshold indicator, smooth transition to reading
**Voice:** "Chapter unlocked. You know the vocab. Now read the story."

### 3. The Recognition
**Feeling:** The "aha" moment - studied vocab appearing in context
**Design:** Subtle highlight or indicator (not disruptive)
**Voice:** Let the recognition speak for itself - minimal UI intervention

### 4. Progress View
**Feeling:** Tangible growth
**Design:** Clear metrics, vocabulary counts, chapters unlocked
**Voice:** "847 words learned. 12 chapters read. Keep going."

## Positioning

### What We Are
- A vocabulary study tool designed specifically for webtoon readers
- Evidence-based learning (FSRS spaced repetition)
- Content-driven: learn the words from chapters you want to read
- For intermediate learners ready for authentic content

### What We're Not
- A comprehensive Korean course (we assume you can read Hangul)
- A webtoon reading platform (we help you prepare to read)
- Gamified entertainment (we're a serious tool with personality)
- For absolute beginners (basic vocab knowledge assumed)

### Competitive Differentiation

**vs KoreanClass101:**
- Focused on reading comprehension, not comprehensive curriculum
- Your chosen content, not generic lessons
- Clean, modern interface vs cluttered lesson marketplace

**vs Lingopie:**
- Structured learning (FSRS) vs passive consumption
- Webtoon-specific (text + visual context)
- You control the content from day one

**vs Readlang:**
- Pre-built vocabulary decks from entire chapters
- FSRS spaced repetition vs basic flashcards
- Designed specifically for Korean webtoons, not general web content
- Study before reading, not just click-to-translate while reading

**vs Anki:**
- Pre-built decks specific to chapters
- Integrated reading experience
- Designed for this exact use case

## User Experience Principles

### Core UX Values

1. **Respect the effort** - Don't trivialize the work with fake gamification
2. **Show clear progress** - Users should always know where they stand
3. **Remove friction** - Between study and reading, between sessions
4. **Honor motivation** - Users came here for webtoons, keep that goal visible
5. **Optimize for return** - Make coming back feel natural, not pressured

### User Journey Touchpoints

**Onboarding:**
- Set expectations: "This takes work, but you'll read webtoons in Korean"
- Select first webtoon quickly
- Begin studying immediately

**Daily Use:**
- Quick access to study session
- Clear progress toward next chapter unlock
- Minimal navigation, maximum focus

**Milestone Moments:**
- First chapter completed in Korean
- 100th word mastered
- First full webtoon series completed
- Let users share these achievements naturally

## Future Considerations

As Toonky grows and adds features (streaks, achievements, community), maintain these core principles:

- **Data-driven recommendations** should feel helpful, not algorithmic
- **Community features** should enhance learning, not distract from it
- **Achievements** should mark real progress, not arbitrary actions
- **Streaks** should encourage consistency without inducing guilt

Remember: Every feature must answer "Does this help users read webtoons in Korean?"

## Brand Guardrails

### Always
- Be honest about effort required
- Connect study to reading goals
- Use clean, readable design
- Respect user intelligence
- Make progress visible and meaningful

### Never
- Over-hype mundane interactions
- Use guilt or pressure tactics
- Sacrifice readability for style
- Treat learning like a game
- Lose sight of the core mission: webtoons in Korean
